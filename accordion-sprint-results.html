Results confirmed your hypothesis

Something worked as expected. This is useful, but it raises a new question. A confirmed hypothesis tells you that a tactic has merit. It does not yet tell you whether it will work consistently, or whether it holds for different segments of your ICP.

Sprint 2 can build on this finding: try the same approach with a larger or slightly different sample, or test a second variable now that the first is established.

Results did not confirm your hypothesis

Something did not work as expected. This is equally useful. The next step is to understand why. Did the channel not reach your ICP? Did conversations stall at a specific point? Did prospects respond but not move forward? Each of these points to a different Sprint 2 focus.

Do not set aside a hypothesis just because it was not confirmed. Look closely at where it broke down.

Results were inconclusive

You did not generate enough interactions to draw a clear conclusion, or the results were too mixed to point in a clear direction. This is common in Sprint 1, especially for founders who are still building their pipeline.

In this case, Sprint 2 may involve running a cleaner version of the same test under better conditions, or narrowing the hypothesis to something more testable given your current pipeline.
