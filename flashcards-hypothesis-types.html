Acquisition hypothesis

Tests how you are finding and reaching qualified prospects

An acquisition hypothesis focuses on the top of your pipeline. It asks whether a specific channel, message, or outreach approach will get the right people into a conversation.

Acquisition hypothesis

For example

"I believe that LinkedIn outreach to operations managers in the manufacturing sector will generate a higher response rate than cold email for my ICP. I will test this by sending 20 LinkedIn messages and 20 cold emails over two weeks and comparing response rates."
"I believe that asking existing customers for referrals will be the fastest way to add qualified leads to my pipeline. I will test this by reaching out to five current customers with a direct referral ask this week."
VS

Conversion hypothesis

Tests how you are moving qualified prospects toward a decision

A conversion hypothesis focuses on what happens once you are in a conversation. It asks whether a specific approach, message, or technique will help a prospect move forward.

Conversion hypothesis

For example

"I believe that leading with the cost of the problem in my discovery conversations will move prospects further toward a decision faster. I will test this in my next five qualified conversations and track whether the prospect asks about next steps unprompted."
"I believe that prospects are stalling because I am not asking for a clear next step at the end of the call. I will test a direct close ask in my next four conversations and note the response."
