Prospecting approach 1 of 4

Outbound Prospecting

Outbound prospecting means initiating contact with potential customers who have not previously expressed interest in your product. Common outbound tactics include cold email, LinkedIn outreach, phone calls, and direct mail.

Outbound works best when you have a tightly defined ICP, because precision is what makes cold outreach feel relevant rather than random. A message sent to exactly the right person at exactly the right time is not cold at all.

Prospecting approach 1 of 4

Outbound Prospecting

Outbound is a good fit when...

  • Your ICP is specific and identifiable (for example, operations managers at manufacturing companies with 50–200 employees)
  • You can personalise your message based on what you know about the prospect
  • You are willing to follow up consistently and professionally

Prospecting approach 2 of 4

Inbound Prospecting

Inbound prospecting means attracting prospects who come to you, usually through content, search, or your online presence. Blog posts, webinars, social media content, and search engine optimisation (SEO) are common inbound tools.

Inbound generates warmer leads because prospects have already shown interest. The trade-off is that inbound takes time to build. It is not a short-term tactic.

Prospecting approach 2 of 4

Inbound Prospecting

Inbound is a good fit when...

  • You can commit to creating consistent, useful content over time
  • Your ICP actively searches for solutions online
  • You want to reduce cost per lead over the long term

Prospecting approach 3 of 4

Referral Prospecting

Referral prospecting means leveraging your existing network and happy customers to generate introductions. A referral from a trusted source dramatically shortens the sales cycle because trust is already established.

Most founders underestimate how much prospecting they can do through referrals, and overestimate how uncomfortable it is to ask. If you have delivered real value to a customer, asking for an introduction is not a burden. It is an opportunity for them to help someone they know.

Prospecting approach 3 of 4

Referral Prospecting

Referral is a good fit when...

  • You have customers who are genuinely satisfied with what you do
  • Your network overlaps with your ICP
  • You are willing to ask directly rather than hinting

Prospecting approach 4 of 4

Community Prospecting

Community prospecting means building relationships through professional networks, industry events, online forums, Slack groups, trade associations, and startup ecosystems. Atlantic Canada has a strong and well-connected entrepreneurial community. This is an asset worth using.

Community prospecting is slower than outbound but generates high-quality relationships. People buy from those they know and trust, and community is where that trust is built over time.

Prospecting approach 4 of 4

Community Prospecting

Community is a good fit when...

  • Your ICP participates in identifiable professional communities
  • You can contribute value before asking for anything in return
  • You are playing a long game alongside shorter-term outbound tactics
